# History of Formula 1

![Cover image – Formula 1 history](images/cover.jpg)

Formula 1 (F1) is the highest class of international single-seater motorsport. Since 1950, it has combined extreme speed, advanced technology, and elite drivers to become a global sport.

---

## Origins of Formula 1 (Before 1950)

![Early Grand Prix racing](images/pre-1950.jpg)

Before Formula 1 officially existed, motor racing took place in European Grand Prix events, often on public roads.  
After World War II, the FIA created a unified set of rules for a world championship.

---

## Birth of the World Championship (1950)

![1950 British Grand Prix at Silverstone](images/1950-silverstone.jpg)

- First Grand Prix: Great Britain (Silverstone)
- First World Champion: Giuseppe Farina
- Front-engine cars
- Very limited safety standards

---

## Technological Evolution (1960s–1970s)

![1960s and 1970s Formula 1 cars](images/1960s-1970s.jpg)

### 1960s
- Engines moved to the rear
- Major aerodynamic developments
- Iconic teams such as Lotus and Ferrari

### 1970s
- Introduction of aerodynamic wings
- Significant safety improvements
- Legendary drivers like Niki Lauda and Jackie Stewart

---

## Turbo Era and Rivalries (1980s)

![Turbo era Formula 1 cars](images/1980s-turbo.jpg)

- Extremely powerful turbo engines
- Rapid commercial and media growth
- Historic rivalry between Ayrton Senna and Alain Prost
- Dominance of McLaren and Williams

---

## Global Expansion and Safety (1990s)

![1990s Formula 1 grid](images/1990s.jpg)

- Expansion to new continents
- Major safety advancements
- Death of Ayrton Senna in 1994
- Rise of Michael Schumacher

---

## Ferrari Dominance (2000–2004)

![Michael Schumacher Ferrari](images/schumacher-ferrari.jpg)

- Five consecutive championships for Schumacher
- Ferrari became the dominant team
- Peak technical and strategic excellence

---

## New Champions (2005–2013)

![Red Bull and Renault era](images/2005-2013.jpg)

- Regulations to balance competition
- Champions included:
  - Fernando Alonso
  - Kimi Räikkönen
  - Sebastian Vettel (four consecutive titles)

---

## Hybrid Era and Mercedes (2014–2020)

![Mercedes hybrid era](images/2014-2020.jpg)

- Introduction of V6 hybrid power units
- Focus on efficiency and sustainability
- Mercedes dominance
- Lewis Hamilton reached seven world titles

---

## Modern Formula 1 (2021–Present)

![Modern Formula 1 cars](images/modern-f1.jpg)

- New regulations to improve overtaking
- Introduction of the budget cap
- Growing younger audience
- Recent dominance of Max Verstappen

---

## Conclusion

![Formula 1 podium celebration](images/conclusion.jpg)

Formula 1 has evolved from dangerous road races into a highly technological and global sport, representing the pinnacle of motorsport engineering and competition.

---

## Facts and Trivia

- Ferrari is the only team to compete since 1950  
- A Formula 1 car can accelerate from 0 to 100 km/h in about 2.5 seconds  
- Formula 1 races in more than 20 countries each season
